Image as a link - Web Design

This is a project for the design and development of a commercial website. My team and I first established the website's style and brand positioning in this project. It is a luxury jewelry website with a minimalist design approach. Once the main tone of the website was determined, we proceeded to design the interactive elements of the web pages to ensure a consistent and appealing appearance across various devices. The specific development of the website involved using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for layout and animation effects. As for generating images for the website, Photoshop and Figma were used as the primary tools.

The website underwent three significant iterations, from its initial concept to the final product. We boldly poured our ideas into creating the website's style guide and a few basic pages during the first iteration. The layout of these pages was not mature, lacked balance, contained excessive white space, and lacked animation and interactivity.

Some of these issues were discovered and addressed during the second iteration, while others were modified and perfected in the final major iteration. During the second iteration, we added a popup feedback feature after users submitted forms and took inspiration from numerous luxury and jewelry brand websites to maintain a clean and tidy homepage. We decided to use a classical painting as the main image for the homepage and designed a simple layout. However, we later realized that a single image on the homepage looked too sparse.

Therefore, we incorporated a video on the website's homepage in the third iteration. After extensive searching on free video websites, we found a video that best matched the brand name and vibe. To ensure adaptive design with the video background, we converted the video into a webp format image animation. We were pleased with the result. In the final iteration, we fixed the form to make it more user-friendly and added a carousel effect to the product showcase page.

Image as a link - Application UI Design

This is a project aimed at designing a health app specifically for the elderly population. The project utilizes various technologies, including Figma, Photoshop, and ProtoPie. In the initial stages, the team conducted research on the smartphone usage patterns of people aged 75 and above, focusing on health-related apps. The team identified the primary objectives and pain points of elderly users when using health apps. After summarizing the user journey, specific solutions were developed to address potential issues users might encounter and designed the features to meet old people's needs. The team then created detailed storyboards to visualize the scenarios.

Once the ideas were organized, team members meticulously considered colour schemes, fonts, and UI layouts. After final decisions were made, the software's wireframes were created in Figma.

After obtaining the wireframes, we further categorized and organized the elements, moving them to ProtoPie for refinement. During this process, we paid close attention to what interactions and actions elderly users preferred and found most convenient with the least error probability. Once the software was refined on ProtoPie, we contacted our research participants again and requested their involvement in software testing. During the final iteration, we analyzed the test results and made improvements to the software's functionalities. The end product is showcased in the video.

Fonts

FKRasterRomanCompact-Blended for headings

Geomanist for paragraphs

I have chosen FKRasterRomanCompact-Blended as the heading font for its compact and modern tech-inspired style, which captures the audience's attention and highlights the professional image of my portfolio. As for the paragraph text, I opted for Geomanist, a clean and clear sans-serif font that ensures excellent readability and a comfortable reading experience. This font combination effectively emphasizes the characteristics of my digital media and design works, ensuring an outstanding presentation of both visual and content aspects in my portfolio.

Art direction

I chose black and yellow as the main color scheme to emphasize the novelty and avant-garde nature of digital media. Additionally, I sought a pixelated integrated font to complement the design. To achieve this, I searched for posters with a similar style as a reference.
