Media Academic Research

This research aims to explore the causes of mindless consumption behaviour during the COVID-19 pandemic and the role of the media in influencing negative mental states and irrational consumption behaviour triggered by sudden public health events. It is a collaborative effort between a four-person team and is scheduled for completion by the Spring of 2022.

Quantitative Research Design

The quantitative research design involves two distinct studies conducted by separate research teams. The first study by Hu, Yue-Qian, Xie Piao, Wang Ying, Huang Zhi-Xin, Wu Yi-Ting, and Sun Hai-Long from the School of Business at Guangdong University of Foreign Studies in China aimed to investigate the relationship between Negative Mentality and Irrational Consumption Behavior. This study employed an online scale-questions questionnaire administered to 466 Chinese participants. The independent variable in this study was Negative Mentality, while the dependent variable was Irrational Consumption Behavior.

The second study, conducted by Bentall RP, Lloyd A, Bennett K, McKay R, Mason L, et al., focused on Pandemic Buying and tested a psychological model of over-purchasing and panic buying during the early phase of the COVID-19 pandemic. This study involved participants from the United Kingdom and the Republic of Ireland, with an online scale-questions questionnaire distributed to 2025 participants from each country. The study's independent variable included Depression and Generalized Anxiety Level, and the dependent variable was Over-purchasing. Control variables such as age, gender, and income were also considered in the analysis.

Both studies utilized questionnaire questions that were based on existing literature to ensure accuracy and precision. The collected data underwent reanalysis using SPSS to calculate correlation coefficients, p-values, and scatter diagrams, providing valuable insights into the relationships between the variables under investigation.

Qualitative Research Design

The qualitative research design for this study involves a discourse analysis of the top 20 Quora answers. This approach was chosen due to the belief that individuals are more inclined to express their authentic emotions and opinions on the Quora platform. By analyzing the content of these top-rated responses, the research aims to gain deeper insights into people's genuine thoughts and perceptions regarding the topic under investigation. This qualitative approach provides an opportunity to explore the nuances of participants' attitudes and experiences in relation to the research focus, ultimately contributing to a richer understanding of the phenomenon.

Research Conclusion

The analysis of data from both China and England & Ireland supports the hypothesis that negative mentality significantly and positively correlates with irrational consumption during the pandemic (P-value less then 0.01, indicating a significant correlation; |r|> 0). Notably, this influence of negative emotions on irrational consumption is notably more pronounced in China (r = 0.568) compared to Ireland (r = 0.28) and England (r = 0.253).

Furthermore, the study reveals that control variables including age, gender, and income do not exert a discernible impact on irrational consumption behaviors during the COVID-19 period. Among the findings, social psychological factors emerge as the most frequently cited contributors to irrational consumption, while coping behavior is not identified as a primary driver of excessive purchasing. The study suggests that misinformation and rumors propagated by news media play a significant role in amplifying public panic and anxiety, thereby influencing shifts in consumer behavior.
